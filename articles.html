Why Accessibility in Storyline
Starts with Slide Architecture

Most Storyline developers bolt accessibility on at the end — adding alt text just before publishing and hoping WCAG checkers don't catch anything serious. After building dozens of ADA and Section 508-compliant courses, here's what I've learned: the moment you think about accessibility as a finishing step, you've already failed. It has to be the first architectural decision.

Tab order, focus management, colour contrast, keyboard navigation — none of these can be reliably fixed after a course is built. They must be designed in from the master slide level. This article walks through exactly how I structure Storyline templates to make accessibility the default, not the exception.
