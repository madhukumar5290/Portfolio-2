Case Study 01
Compliance · Banking · Interactive Scenarios

Anti-Money Laundering

Storyline 360Branching ScenariosSCORMBanking

The Challenge

Compliance training in banking is typically dry, slide-heavy, and forgotten within days. The challenge was to make AML regulations genuinely engaging — and to ensure learners could apply the knowledge to real-world detection scenarios, not just recall definitions.

The Approach

I designed a fully branching scenario-based course where learners take the role of a compliance officer reviewing suspicious transactions. Each decision leads to a different outcome — with immediate, contextual feedback grounded in real regulatory frameworks.

"End-to-end course design: storyboard, visual design, interaction architecture, and SCORM packaging — all in-house."

Case Study 02
Accessibility · ADA/508 · Custom Interaction

Drag & Drop with Full Keyboard Accessibility

Storyline 360JavaScriptADA/508Tab Order

The Challenge

Most Storyline drag-and-drop interactions are completely inaccessible to keyboard users. The default behaviour ignores tab order, provides no keyboard activation, and excludes an entire segment of learners.

The Approach

Built a drag-and-drop interaction with custom JavaScript to enforce logical tab order, keyboard activation via Enter/Space, and visible focus indicators — all meeting WCAG 2.1 AA and Section 508 requirements. Accessibility was the architecture, not an afterthought.

"Most developers add alt text at the end and call it done. I build accessibility in from the first trigger."

Case Study 03
Flash-to-HTML5 · Conversion · Modern Delivery

Flash-to-HTML5 Conversion: Personal SWOT

HTML5StorylineMobileLegacy Conversion

The Challenge

With Flash reaching end-of-life, organisations faced a crisis: large course libraries built in Flash, rendering on zero modern devices. The challenge was not just conversion — it was preserving the original interaction fidelity and learning design intent.

The Approach

Rebuilt the Personal SWOT course in Storyline 360 with full HTML5 output — responsive layout, touch-friendly interactions, and identical learning flow to the original. Mobile and tablet-ready from day one.
